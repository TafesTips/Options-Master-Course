Wheel Strategy

Case Study #3: Amazon (AMZN) Income Wheel Strategy

Market Context (2023-2024): After a challenging 2022, Amazon entered a period of consolidation and recovery in 2023-2024. The stock traded in a range between $90 and $150 for much of this period, creating ideal conditions for the Wheel Strategy, which thrives in range-bound markets.

The Strategy: The Complete Income Wheel

An investor implemented the full Wheel Strategy on Amazon, cycling between cash secured puts and covered calls to generate continuous income regardless of market direction.

Starting Capital

$100,000

Strategy

Full Wheel Strategy

Timeframe

30-day cycles

Total Return

43.2%

  1. Phase 1: Cash Secured Puts - Sell puts until assignment occurs
  2. Phase 2: Covered Calls - Once assigned, sell calls against the shares
  3. Phase 3: Return to Puts - If called away, return to selling puts
  4. Repeat the Cycle - Continue rotating between puts and calls
January 2023 - Initial Cash Secured Put

Market Condition: AMZN trading at $95 after 2022 decline

Action: Sold 10 contracts of $90 cash secured puts (5% OTM) expiring in 30 days for $3.84 per share

February 2023 - Second Cash Secured Put

March 2023 - First Covered Call

April 2023 - Second Covered Call

Your Path to Implementation: Lessons from the Case Studies

These case studies demonstrate the remarkable versatility and power of options income strategies across different market conditions and with various Magnificent 7 stocks. Now, let's extract the key lessons that you can apply to your own portfolio:

1. Match the Strategy to the Market Condition

  • Bull Markets: Use covered calls with higher strike prices (8-10% OTM) to balance income with appreciation potential
  • Bear Markets: Implement cash secured puts with progressively lower strikes to build positions at favorable prices
  • Sideways Markets: Deploy the full Wheel Strategy to generate continuous income regardless of direction
  • Volatile Markets: Focus on shorter-term options (7-14 days) to capitalize on elevated premiums

2. Adapt Your Approach to Each Stock's Characteristics

  • Higher Volatility Stocks (NVDA, TSLA): Use wider strike prices (8-10% OTM) to accommodate larger price swings
  • Lower Volatility Stocks (AAPL, MSFT): Use tighter strike prices (3-5% OTM) to maximize premium capture
  • Growth-Focused Stocks (AMZN, GOOGL): Emphasize covered calls during consolidation periods, cash secured puts during pullbacks
  • Mature Stocks (AAPL, MSFT): Implement more consistent, systematic approaches like the Wheel Strategy

3. Capitalize on Special Situations

  • Earnings Announcements: Sell options 1-2 weeks before earnings to capture elevated implied volatility
  • Market Corrections: Implement laddered cash secured puts to build positions at progressively better prices
  • Sector-Specific News: Sell options on stocks affected by sector news when volatility spikes
  • Major Product Launches: Adjust strike prices to accommodate potential significant moves
